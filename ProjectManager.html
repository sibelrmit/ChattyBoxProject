Project Manager

A Project Manager is someone who is in charge of planning, organising, directing and overseeing the completion of certain projects within a specified timeframe and budget. This is a senior role that requires interaction with many different stakeholders and interactions with all levels of the organisation. They are in charge of organising tasks, managing the team and the budget. This role requires at least an Associate’s Degree, some level of experience within the field (usually at least 5 years’ experience) and depending on the organisation, may sometimes require certification as well. The main areas of study relating to a Project Manager are: Engineering, IT/Computer Science, Business/Business Administration and Management.

The skills required for this position are leadership skills as the person will be leading numerous people to work together to complete the assigned project and money management skills as they will be required to complete the project with a set budget. A Project Manager will also be required to have exceptional writing skills as the project will be needed to be documented thoroughly from start to finish. Other skills include time management skills as meeting deadlines in a project is mandatory and non-negotiable. They are required to have the ability to manage high stress situations, multi-tasking, attention to detail as well as conflict resolution skills.

The majority of the roles as a Project Manager is full time working around 40 hours per week however, it is not uncommon for a Project Manager to be working over 40 hours weekly.
