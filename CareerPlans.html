Career Plans Analysed

The ideal jobs within our team vary greatly, but all have at least part of their foundation based within the IT industry. Most of the jobs listed are focused on Information Technology, though certain job titles have an alternative primary root. For instance, one of our team members is interested in the position of Creative Director/Designer within the Games Industry, which has a significant involvement of Design and Arts as well as Information Technology.

Most of the jobs listed have a core focus that falls under one of the following categories: Analysis, particularly of data, and Development of Technology. Therefore, there are many skills in common amongst those that are demanded of all of the job titles here.

Skills that are notably important to have in all of the jobs include:

- Fluency in various languages, such as those involved in manipulation and structuring of databases such as SQL, and those involved in programming and software development, such as Python.

- Fluency in spoken and written languages.

Some job titles, including business analyst, data analyst, and data visualist, have certain common skills including:

- Effective data analysing, either from the mathematical aspect or in familiarity with programs that can process data and turn it into information.

- Familiarity with data visualisation/communication programs.

A unique skill that has been taken note of is fluency as a visual designer, and sufficient artistic proficiency in order to communicate those designs effectively.

In the short term, all of us have extremely similar overarching goals; to graduate from University and attain our respective degrees. However, our plans during our time as students vary slightly, as some of us are interested in taking on internships during our time at University to deepen our understanding of our respective fields prior to formal inauguration into the IT workforce after graduation.

Some paths include a slight extension to their formal education, as there are career plans that involve the attainment of a postgraduate degree. Other job titles seem to benefit from going directly into the IT workforce,as some of our team members plan to immediately get as deeply involved with their degree of choice in their career as soon as they graduate, by undertaking graduate programs at businesses.

From that point forward, most of our career plans are largely the same, involving employment in an entry level position related to IT in order to attain experience within the industry, before moving upwards to our ideal job in a few years’ time. Some of us appear to be satisfied with staying with our current ideal job for the foreseeable future, while others are interested in diversifying and holding positions in various branches of IT.
