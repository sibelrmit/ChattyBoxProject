Group Reflections

Sibel Jasar

I believe the team worked much better together in this project in comparison to the last! We definitely communicated a lot more for this project! We had weekly meetings every week, without fail, and for group members that were unable to make it to meetings, we filled them in on new tasks and information. As a group, we got to demonstrate and use our creativity, which was seen with the mock ups and designs we made. It was really fun and cool designing the chatbot together and brainstorming ideas for the chatbots design.

Although we were consistent with the meetings every week, we didn’t have the whole team present for them, but rather usually 3 to 4 members present at each meeting. The first meeting had every member present, but it fell off through project progression. Despite this though, we still improved in the fact that we actually had weekly meetings where we worked on the project together and this really aided the workload and communication for this project!

I was very surprised at the amount of people who were willing to jump in to help other people with their tasks if they asked for it. I can’t really think of anything in specific that could be improved because I think that how my group handled the assignment was how an ideal group is supposed to work. One thing I learnt about groups was that to work well you have to keep an open mind to work well with others.

Group Reflection

As a group, we believed we performed much better in this assignment than our previous, even with the increasingly difficult tasks and challenges that came this time around. As we grew much more comfortable with each other and our communication improved, so did our quality of work. We did not hesitate to help each other when the tasks were a bit overwhelming. We were able to hold successful team meetings each and every week at the same time. We communicated much more often and more frequently this time. It was great to see the amount of teamwork and effort that each individual had put in and we could not be any prouder of what we have achieved as a group.

As initially mentioned, we definitely encountered many more challenges for this assignment. Such challenges included working within the agreed timeframe. Due to the nearing end of semester and increase in pressure, stress and workload for this course as well as for other courses, we encountered some tasks requiring more time than our agreed date. However, due to us setting early deadlines, this gave us enough time to finish our tasks and make any necessary adjustments without rushing to complete the assignment. We also all proactively offered to help our team members who were struggling with their assigned tasks which was great to see. Another challenge we had was trying to get our prototype working. With our limited experience in coding and using software that did not have the available features that we needed, we were able to come up with alternative solutions as a team to counteract this.

Although we did have weekly meetings, we weren’t able to get everyone to attend. However, this wasn’t a major issue for us as we had alternative methods to fill everyone in who missed out. We recorded the meetings, and messaged our team members on what was discussed including any tasks we have assigned to them.

With the increased workload and challenges that we had faced this time, it had helped us learn how to overcome these challenges and grow not only individually but as well as in a team which turned out to be a great learning experience for each of us. Additionally, with the COVID-19 restrictions, it had also taught us how to adapt to a new environment since we were always used to working face-to-face in groups rather than a work from home setting.
